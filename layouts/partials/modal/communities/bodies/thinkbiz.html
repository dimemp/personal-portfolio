Description

In 2012, I joined ThinkBiz as a founding member to help launch the first student entrepreneurship club in Greece at AUEB. My first few months were spent in the trenches with a great team. We didn’t just talk about startups; we worked to deconstruct them. We organized pitching events for local teams, and by analyzing the common hurdles and questions that surfaced, we began building a "Knowledge Base" to serve as a practical roadmap for anyone looking to start a company from scratch.

As the organization grew, I moved into an HR role, managing the internal culture and organizational structure for our 40+ members. During that same period, I led a team of seven to launch the “Day in a Startup” initiative. We coordinated exclusive, small-group visits to the offices of Greek tech leaders like Skroutz and PeoplePerHour, giving students a rare chance to see how a high-growth startup actually operates day-to-day. For me, ThinkBiz was about creating those direct links between students and the local ecosystem, ensuring we provided real-world value rather than just academic theory.
