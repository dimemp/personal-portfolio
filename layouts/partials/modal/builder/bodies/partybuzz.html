Description

Partybuzz was my first deep dive into the Greek startup ecosystem. More than just a simple RSVP tool, we built a platform for crowdsourcing home party logistics. This allowed organizers to request specific in-kind or financial contributions from guests to help fund their parties. We also developed a unique "Group RSVP" feature, which was a first at the time. This feature enabled users to join parties as a collective, meaning the host had to accept the entire group or none at all.

Built with Django, JS, jQuery, and PostgreSQL, I led a team of two developers and managed the branding (except the logo) and web design. This project anticipated the "collaborative social" trend currently dominated by apps like Partiful (which has raised $27.3M, including a Series A led by a16z).
