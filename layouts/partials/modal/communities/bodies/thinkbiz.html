Description

I joined ThinkBiz Hellas at its founding in late 2012 as a member of the Knowledge Base project, an initiative focused on capturing founder Q&As and converting them into reusable venture-building resources. The work was straightforward but formative: co-organizing 6-7 pitching events that brought together roughly 150 participants in total, including serving as Head Organizer for a flagship event with 60+ attendees. It was my first direct exposure to the mechanics of how early-stage founder communities actually function.

From there I moved into a project leadership role, running "Day in Startup", a project designed to give students direct immersion into the day-to-day operations of real tech companies. I led an amazing team of 7 to organize 5 visits to top-tier Greek tech companies, including Skroutz and Daily Secret, with 40+ participants. The insight that shaped our project's approach: proximity to working teams teaches more in an afternoon than a semester of theory.

Alongside the projects work, I spent two years on the HR and talent side of the organization, co-designing recruitment processes, running interviews, and placing new members into projects that matched their strengths. The internal upskilling workshops we ran were a natural extension of the same logic: an organization that doesn't systematically transfer knowledge between members loses it every time someone leaves. These early experiences with team design and internal operations laid the groundwork for how I later influenced the structure of teams at Founderhood and Mindspace.
